Dr.APJ Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Biography

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, India, was a renowned Indian scientist and the 11th President of India. He earned a degree in aerospace engineering and played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development.
Dr. Kalam's illustrious career at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) saw him contributing significantly to the successful development of the Satellite Launch Vehicle and Agni and Prithvi missiles. His leadership was instrumental in making India a self-reliant nation in the field of missile technology.
Throughout his life, he advocated for the importance of education and research in the progress of a nation. Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, but his legacy as a visionary scientist and a beloved leader continues to inspire countless individuals, especially the youth, to dream big and work diligently towards a brighter future for India.
